Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the UNT?

The Unified National Test (UNT) is a mandatory exam for high school graduates in Kazakhstan, used for university admission.

2. What subjects are included in the UNT?

Core subjects: Mathematical Literacy, Reading Literacy, and History of Kazakhstan. Additionally, two profile subjects depend on the chosen major.

3. How many points are needed to enter a university?

The minimum threshold is 50 points, but some majors and universities require higher scores, such as 65–90 points.

4. How many times can you take the UNT?

The UNT can be taken multiple times throughout the year: in the main session, retake sessions, and additional attempts in August and January.

5. What if I don’t pass the minimum threshold?

You can retake the exam during the next available session or enroll in a college and later transfer to a university.
