Kazakhstan to Introduce Adaptive Testing for National University Entrance Exam (ENT) by 2025 The Ministry of Education and Science of Kazakhstan has announced plans to implement adaptive testing for the National University Entrance Exam (ENT) starting in 2025. This new system aims to tailor the difficulty of questions to the performance of each student in real-time. According to officials, adaptive testing will create a fairer and more personalized experience, allowing students to demonstrate their true abilities without unnecessary stress. "This approach ensures that students are neither overwhelmed by excessively difficult questions nor under-challenged by easier ones," said a ministry spokesperson. The reform is part of broader efforts to modernize the education system and align it with international standards. Pilot testing will begin next year, giving students and teachers time to adapt to the new format.

The ministry also noted that traditional multiple-choice sections will remain part of the test but will be complemented by the adaptive system to assess studentsâ€™ critical thinking and problem-solving skills more effectively.
